üìò 1. What is the Kanban System?

The Kanban system is a visual inventory management method that ensures consumables (such as drills, gloves, cleaning cloths, etc.) are always available without excess inventory or searching time.

  • The system works according to the two-bin principle:
  • Each item has two identical bins.
  • One bin is in use.
  • When this bin becomes empty, it is turned upside down and placed on the Kanban cart.
  • The facilitator replenishes all empty bins weekly.

üìç 2. Location of the System

  • The central Kanban rack is located in the middle of the department, allowing both Milling and Bench operators easy access.
  • Next to the rack is the Kanban cart ‚Äì this is where all empty bins are collected.

üß© 3. Structure of the System

Each consumable item has:

  • Two bins, each clearly labeled with:
  • Stock number
  • Picture of the item
  • Bin location
  • Reorder quantity (Q)

üì¶ 4.1 Taking Material

  • Go to the Kanban cabinet.
  • Locate the correct item.
  • Take the consumable from the front bin (always use the front bin first).
  • Place the bin back in position after taking what you need.
‚ö†Ô∏è Important: Never take items from the second bin while the first bin still contains stock.

üóëÔ∏è 4.2 When a Bin Is Empty

  • Verify that the bin is completely empty.
  • Turn the empty bin upside down.
  • Place the empty bin on the Kanban cart.
  • Take the second (full) bin from the rack and continue working.
üí° Tip: Check that the label remains readable and firmly attached. If not, report it to the facilitator.

üö´ 4.3 What Not to Do

  • Do not take extra stock outside the Kanban system.
  • Do not forget to place the empty bin on the Kanban cart.

‚ùì 4.4 Item Needed That Is Not in the Kanban System

  • Check that the item is indeed not available in the Kanban rack.
  • Fill in the order request sheet located next to the Kanban cabinet.
  • Record the stock number and quantity.
  • Inform the facilitator, so the item can be ordered or considered for inclusion in the Kanban system.

üë∑‚Äç‚ôÇÔ∏è 5. Role of the Facilitator

The Tier 5 facilitator is responsible for maintaining, replenishing, and ordering all consumables.

5.1 Facilitator Responsibilities

  • Responsible for maintaining, inspecting, ordering, and replenishing all consumable bins.
  • After each Tier 5 meeting, ask if there are any comments or issues regarding consumables.

5.2 Ordering Moment

  • The ordering moment takes place every Wednesday, before the Tier 4 meeting.
  • Collect all empty bins from the Kanban cart and any order requests from the request sheet.
  • Check each Kanban bin for:
    • Stock number and required quantity.
    • Place all orders in MRN before the Tier 4 meeting.
  • Discuss any questions or remarks during the Tier 4 meeting.

5.3 Replenishment / Pick-Up

  • After the Tier 5 meeting, go to the warehouse.
  • Take the Kanban cart with you if needed.
  • Collect all ordered items and fill the bins with the correct stock numbers.
  • Double-check that the quantities are correct.
  • Place the newly filled bins back in the rack, positioning them at the back behind the bin currently in use.
  • Items that are not part of the Kanban system should be placed in the designated area.
